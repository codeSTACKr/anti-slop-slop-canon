# Warranted uncertainty control

## Context

Review a direct field observation that uses precise uncertainty because the available evidence is incomplete.

## Input

The seal may be leaking near the lower hinge. We found water below that corner, but the frame was dry when we opened it.

## Expected behavior

Leave the passage unchanged. Calm confidence does not require deleting a warranted hedge or turning an observation into a diagnosis.

## Assertions

- Preserve `may be leaking` and the distinction between the wet corner and dry frame.
- Do not claim that the seal caused the water.
- Preserve both sentence lengths and their order.
- Treat no mutation as a successful result.
