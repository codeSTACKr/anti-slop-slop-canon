### Voice summary

Write with calm confidence in plain language. Ground claims in specific facts. Sound
conversational and capable. Use dry humor lightly. Preserve the writer's meaning and
character.

### Non-negotiable preferences

- Preserve meaning, facts, uncertainty, and intent. Never invent evidence, benefits, or
  enthusiasm.
- Preserve exact quotations, code, structured data, and legally fixed wording byte for
  byte. They are exempt from all style rules.
- Make the smallest useful change. Leave clean prose alone. Preserve effective images,
  fragments, cadence, and warranted hedges.
- In mutable prose, never use an em dash, en dash, semicolon, exclamation point, or
  mid-sentence colon. A colon may introduce a block, list, label, or code sample.

### Patterns to avoid

- No hype, marketing jargon, unsupported enthusiasm, vague praise, or filler
  intensifiers.
- No "here's the thing" or close variants.
- No contrast framing built from formulas such as "not X, but Y," "not just X, also Y,"
  or "until now, but now." State the point directly. Factual comparison is allowed.
- No canned openers or closers such as "in today's world," "it's important to note,"
  "let's delve into," "in conclusion," or "at the end of the day."
- No stock transitions such as "furthermore," "moreover," "that being said," "at its
  core," or "it's worth noting." Connect ideas through their meaning.
